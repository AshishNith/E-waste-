Environmental Impact

E-waste contains hazardous materials that can contaminate the environment if not disposed of properly. Heavy metals like lead, mercury, and cadmium can leach into the soil and water, posing serious risks to ecosystems and human health.

Health Risks

Improper handling of e-waste can lead to health issues for workers in informal recycling sectors. Exposure to toxic substances can result in respiratory problems, skin diseases, and other serious health conditions.

Urgency of the Issue

With the rapid growth of technology, e-waste is one of the fastest-growing waste streams in the world. It is crucial to raise awareness and take action to mitigate its impacts on the environment and public health.
